Robert Baden-Powell
Founder of the world Scout movement

  • 1857 On February 22 born in Paddington, London, England
  • 1876 Joined the British army and appointed as Sub-lieutenant for the 13th Hussars in Lucknow, India, and specialised in scouting, map-making and reconnaissance
  • 1899 Declared a hero for successfully defending the Mafeking Town during the Boer War and was promoted to Major-General by Queen Victoria
  • 1907 Held a seven-day experimental camp for 20 boys on Brownsea Island, Dorset, England
  • 1910 Retired from the British army as Lieutenant General and focused on the Scout Movement
  • 1912 Married Olave St Clair Soames and subsequently had three children: Arthur, Heather and Betty
  • 1913 Started Gilwell Park Training Centre for Scout Leaders in London, England
  • 1920 Acclaimed Chief Scout of the World at the 1st World Scout Jamboree in London, England
  • 1925 Opened the International Scout Chalet in Kandersteg, Switzerland (now known as the Kandersteg International Scout Centre)
  • 1929 Made Lord Baden-Powell, 1st Baron of Gilwell, by King George V
  • 1937 Built and lived at his home, Paxtu cottage, in Nyeri, Kenya
  • 1941 On January 08 died
